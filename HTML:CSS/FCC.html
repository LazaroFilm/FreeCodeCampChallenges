Balancing the rig

You need to balance the camera perfectly in order to take full advantage of the system. A good balance is critical to a good operating.

Before even attempting to balance your rig you need to make sure that you have all the parts you will be flying on the sled, including all the accessories, all cables, batteries and memory cards. It is possible to add or remove items after, but you will need to trim the balance to compensate for the weight shift.

Don Juan, Missionary, High mode, Low Mode

There are may way you can use your Steadicam to get the shot, it's important to train in every configuration possible.

With a Steadicam, the camera is flying freely next to your body, that allows you to move it around and turn it into positions that would not be possible on the shoulder. There are multiple positions:

  • Missionary, with the camera facing forward
  • Don Juan, with the camera facing behind you
  • High mode, where the camera is on top of the sled
  • Low mode, where the camera is at the bottom.

You can switch between missionary and don juan without modifying your sled balance, however, you will need to adjust your sled between high and low modes.
